Tinolang Manok

Home

Description

Tinolang manok is a staple in Filipino homes and reflects the country’s love for simple, nourishing food. It relies on affordable ingredients yet delivers deep comfort in every spoonful.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 piece small onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (chopped)
  • 1 piece ginger (cut into strips)
  • 500 grams chicken (cut into 8 pieces)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 pieces Knorr Chicken Cubes
  • 1 piece sayote or small green papaya (sliced)
  • 2 stalks malunggay (leaves picked)

Steps

  1. Sauté the Aromatics Heat a pot over medium heat, then add oil. Add onions, garlic, and ginger. Sauté for about 2 minutes or until fragrant and slightly softened.
  2. Brown the Chicken Add chicken pieces to the pot and sear on all sides until the skin is lightly browned. Work in batches if needed to avoid overcrowding.
  3. Simmer the Broth Pour in the water and add the Knorr Chicken Broth cubes. Stir to dissolve, then bring to a gentle simmer until the chicken becomes tender and fully cooked. Tip: Skim off any foam that rises to the surface to keep the broth clear.
  4. Cook the Vegetables Add the sayote or green papaya and cook until tender but not mushy. Tip: The vegetables are ready when you can easily pierce them with a fork.
  5. Finish with the Greens Add the malunggay leaves and simmer for 1 minute or just until wilted. Serve hot. Tip: Malunggay is rich in vitamins and minerals and is often enjoyed by nursing moms and children; you can also use dahon ng sili as an alternative.
